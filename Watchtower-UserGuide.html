The Watchtower Philosophy

Watchtower is not a prediction machine. It is a situational awareness terminal built to help you think more clearly before you act.

“Watchtower does not tell you what to do. It shows you where to look, what has happened historically, and what conditions currently exist — so you can make your own informed decision.”

Every feature is designed to slow you down, inform your decision, and remind you of your own plan — without telling you what to do. The app observes. You decide.

Three core principles behind every feature:

  • Observation over prediction — patterns are tendencies, not guarantees
  • Discipline over impulse — your plan matters more than today’s price
  • Survivability over maximization — staying in the game is the edge
Watchtower was built around a cash-only, no-leverage philosophy. Maximum direct loss is always limited to capital invested. No margin calls. No broker-forced liquidation. If you can’t afford the trade, you don’t make it.
“Compounding only works if you stay solvent and emotionally stable long enough to continue participating.”

Getting Started

Setup

Watchtower requires a free Finnhub API key for live price data. No other accounts are needed.

StepAction
1Go to finnhub.io and create a free account
2Copy your API key from the Finnhub dashboard
3Open Watchtower and enter your API key on the setup screen
4Tap CONNECT — your key is stored locally on your device only, never sent to any other server
Your API key is stored in your browser’s local storage only. It is never sent to any server other than Finnhub directly. Do not share your API key with others.

Sparkline Chart

The mini price chart shows the last 30 days of closing prices. Color tells the story at a glance:

Wave Score

A score from 0 to 99 measuring how consistent and repeatable the ticker’s price cycle has been over the last year of data.

Score RangeInterpretation
80–99Consistent historical rhythm observed. Historical cycles have been repeatable and usable for timing awareness
60–79Moderate rhythm. Some consistency but macro events have disrupted the pattern at times
40–59Weak-to-moderate. Use with additional context and significant caution
0–39Weak or irregular. Price behavior driven more by external events than internal cycles. Difficult to time reliably
A high Wave Score reflects historical pattern consistency only. It does not predict future behavior. Any external event — earnings, macro shock, Fed announcement — can break a rhythm instantly.

Logging a Dividend

Select DIVIDEND from the Action dropdown. The form adjusts automatically:

  • Price field becomes Total Received ($) — enter the full dollar amount (e.g. $75.00)
  • Shares field becomes Shares Held — how many shares you held at payment

Dividends appear in Trade History with a gold left border. When you complete a cycle (log a SELL OUT), dividends received during the hold period are automatically added to your total return calculation.

When NOT To Trade

Knowing when to step back is as valuable as knowing when to enter. Restraint is intelligence.

Watchtower includes a pre-trade checklist in the Trade Journal tab. Before logging any trade, run through the checklist. If flags appear, the system does not stop you — but it asks the question your emotions may be avoiding.

A high Wave Score and a favorable signal do not override poor personal conditions. The market will have another setup. A bad decision made in the wrong mental state is harder to recover from than a missed opportunity.

The 10 Conditions

#ConditionWhy It Matters
1Feeling emotional, anxious, or upsetEmotional states impair risk assessment and cause overreaction to normal price movement
2Trying to recover a recent loss quicklyRevenge trading is one of the most common ways small losses become large ones. The market does not owe you a recovery
3Entering because of FOMOFear of missing out is not a thesis. If you cannot articulate why the setup is valid beyond "it’s going up," wait
4Chasing a move that already happenedEntering after a large move has already occurred dramatically changes the risk/reward profile from what the scanner detected
5Position would be too large relative to total capitalOversizing a position makes it psychologically impossible to hold through normal volatility. You will exit at the worst time
6Ignoring your own invalidation pointIf you have already mentally moved your stop, you are not following your plan — you are rationalizing. Patience becomes denial
7No clear entry, target, and invalidation plannedEntering without a plan is not trading. It is gambling with extra steps. Complete your thesis first
8Sleep deprived or not thinking clearlyCognitive impairment affects risk perception the same way alcohol does. If you are not sharp, your judgment is not reliable
9Major macro news or Fed announcement expected todayAny rhythm pattern can be instantly overridden by a major macro event. High-impact news days have elevated volatility and unpredictable direction
10Someone else is pressuring you to make this tradeOther people’s conviction about your money is not a valid thesis. You are the one who will live with the outcome

How the Checklist Works in the App

The checklist appears at the top of the Trade Journal tab, above the log form. Tap any condition that applies to you right now. The result updates automatically:

ResultMeaning
✓ CONDITIONS CLEARNo flags raised. If your thesis is sound and your plan is ready, you may proceed with discipline
⚠ 1 FLAG — CAUTIONAcknowledge the condition consciously before proceeding. Consider whether it can be resolved first
⚠ MULTIPLE FLAGS — HIGH CAUTIONOdds of an emotional or impulsive decision are elevated. Strongly consider waiting
⛔ MULTIPLE FLAGS — DO NOT TRADE NOWStep away. Revisit your thesis when conditions clear and return with a plan, not an impulse
The checklist does not prevent you from making a trade. Watchtower never tells you what to do. It asks the questions you might avoid asking yourself. The decision is always yours.

Restraint as Strategy

The traders who survive long enough to compound are rarely the ones who make the most trades. They are the ones who skip the bad ones.

Every time you run the checklist and it comes back clear, you have confirmed your discipline. Every time it raises a flag and you choose to wait, you have protected your capital from yourself — which is often the hardest thing to do in markets.

“The market will have another setup. A missed opportunity is recoverable. An impulsive loss may not be.”

Using the Data Together

Watchtower is most powerful when multiple signals align independently. This is called convergence.

The Convergence Method

Before entering a trade, check each layer. The more that agree, the stronger the setup:

#LayerWhat to Look For
1Macro EnvironmentAre conditions favorable? (Weakening dollar + geopolitical pressure = bullish for metals)
2Rhythm ScannerWave Score above 70? Signal showing NEAR CYCLE LOW? Consistency above 70%?
3Post-Dividend WindowFor KSLV/KGLD: Are we in the post-div window with historically positive avg?
4Support & ResistanceIs price near support? Or breaking through resistance (bullish)?
5Bollinger Bands (external)On TradingView: Is price near the lower band? Is the SMA above or below current price?
6Your ThesisDoes this setup match what you planned? Or are you chasing because of FOMO?
Real example: KSLV Wave Score 96, Near Cycle Low signal, Post-Div window active with +6.58% historical avg, SR showing near support, lower Bollinger Band holding, and planned entry price reached. Five independent signals agreeing = high-context setup. This is how the app was designed to be used.

Glossary

TermDefinition
Ex-Dividend DateThe date you must own shares before in order to receive the dividend. Price typically adjusts downward by the dividend amount on this date
Return of Capital (ROC)A portion of a dividend that is actually returning your own invested capital rather than generating new income. Reduces your cost basis over time
Covered-Call ETFAn ETF that sells call options on its holdings to generate income paid as a dividend. KSLV and KGLD are both covered-call ETFs. Distributions may include ROC
NAV ErosionWhen the net asset value of an ETF declines over time. High yield can mask NAV erosion if the dividend exceeds actual income generated
Wave ScoreWatchtower’s measure of historical cycle consistency. 0–99. Higher = more repeatable price rhythm
SupportA price level where buying has historically been strong enough to stop or reverse a decline. Multiple touches = stronger support
ResistanceA price level where selling has historically been strong enough to stop or reverse an advance. Multiple touches = stronger resistance
MA7 / MA207-day and 20-day simple moving averages. MA7 above MA20 = bullish short-term trend. MA7 below MA20 = bearish
Invalidation PointThe price at which your original trade thesis is no longer valid. Acts as a mental stop-loss and forces reassessment
Cash OnlyTrading exclusively with settled cash you own. No borrowed money, no margin. Maximum loss always limited to capital invested
Post-Div WindowThe 7-day period after an ex-dividend date. Historically characterized by selling as dividend-capture traders exit
Cycle TroughThe lowest price point in a repeating price cycle. Detected as a local minimum. Potential entry zone
Cycle PeakThe highest price point in a repeating price cycle. Detected as a local maximum. Potential exit zone
ConvergenceWhen multiple independent signals from different parts of Watchtower all point in the same direction. Strengthens the case for a trade
Dividend CaptureThe strategy of buying a stock before its ex-dividend date to receive the dividend, then selling afterward. Risk: post-div price drop can exceed dividend received
BTC MinerA company that generates revenue by mining Bitcoin. Examples: MARA, CLSK. Price correlates strongly with Bitcoin price, often with amplified moves in both directions
